# Session Structure

## Overview

A session is facilitator-led and runs across multiple rounds. Each round has a defined **mode of working**. The same team plays through different modes sequentially, experiencing the contrast firsthand.

**Team size**: minimum 3 players. No upper limit — the facilitator can hire additional team members at any point during the game.

## Modes of Working

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| **Solo with handovers** | Each team member works alone on tasks matching their skills. Work is handed over when it needs a different skill. |
| **Pair work** | Two players work on the same task together. Slower per task, higher quality, potential skill growth. |
| **Mob programming** | The whole team (or a large subset) works on one task at a time. Maximum collaboration, maximum learning, slowest throughput per item but potentially best flow. |

## Round Structure

A round represents one working day. Each day follows this rhythm:

1. **Daily standup**: the team collectively decides what to work on — who picks up which task, whether to collaborate, whether to take over someone else's work
2. **Work phase**: players execute their chosen tasks through dice rolls; metrics update live
3. **End of day**: the facilitator reviews the board with the team before the next standup

The standup is a deliberate moment of team decision-making, not just a status update. It is where the game's key choices happen.

## Facilitator Screen

Visible to everyone on the shared display:

- **Kanban board**: columns for Backlog / In Progress / Done at Story/Task level
- **Flow metrics**: live WIP, lead time, cycle time, throughput per round
- **Quality indicators**: internal quality bar, external quality bar
- **Character panel**: team member names, skill levels, current activity

## Session Arc

A full session progresses through at least two or three rounds with different modes of working. Each round resets to the same starting state — original skill dot grids, original planned vacations, fresh backlog and quality levels. This allows direct comparison between modes under identical conditions.

After each round, players can compare:
- How did lead time change?
- Where did bottlenecks appear?
- How did quality differ?
- How much did skill dots grow?

The facilitator closes with a debrief connecting the game experience to real-world practices (Lean, flow theory, DevOps).

## Session Length

The game simulates one month of work (~20 working days) per round. The pace is adjustable to fit three target formats:

| Format | Duration | Pace |
|---|---|---|
| Demo | 30 minutes | Very fast — decisions in seconds, days fly by |
| Workshop | 1 hour | Moderate — brief standup discussion per day |
| Deep workshop | 2 hours | Slower — richer standup conversations and debrief between rounds |

The core dynamic must be simple enough that standup decisions (who works on what, alone or together) can be made quickly without overthinking — even at high pace. Complexity lives in the outcomes, not the rules.
