# Game Concept

## Overview

A multiplayer serious game for software development teams. Players take on the roles of individual team members building and maintaining **Stagr** — a concert and festival app. The game is designed to be played in a facilitated session — the facilitator introduces the game on a screen and players join via QR code on their phones.

**Stagr** lets users browse lineups, build their festival schedule, buy tickets, and find friends on the festival map. High-stakes moments (tickets selling out, live tracking, payment failures) make quality issues feel real and consequential.

## Educational Goals

The game is designed to create lived experience of key software delivery concepts:

- **Flow**: How work moves through a team from idea to delivery
- **Lead time**: Total time from a request entering the system to delivery
- **Cycle time**: Time a work item is actively being worked on
- **WIP (Work in Progress)**: The cost of carrying too many items at once
- **Throughput**: How much value the team actually delivers over time

A secondary goal is to illustrate the difference between:
- **Specialist teams**: Each person works only in their area of expertise — fast individually, but creates queues at bottlenecks
- **Collaborative/cross-skilling teams**: Team members learn from each other over time, slower at first but smoother flow and higher throughput

## Format

- Facilitator-led session (online)
- Facilitator presents on a shared screen, introduces the game, shows a QR code
- Players join on their phones
- Multiple players, each controlling one team member character

## Core Mechanic Inspiration

Rules inspired by Dungeons & Dragons:
- Characters have skill scores across multiple disciplines
- Dice rolls determine task completion speed, skill checks, leveling up
- Randomness simulates real-world uncertainty in delivery
