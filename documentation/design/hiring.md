# Hiring

## Triggering a Hire

The facilitator triggers a hiring round — either after a team member quits or is fired, or proactively to grow the team. A QR code appears on the facilitator's screen. New participants scan it and roll a character, generating a fresh dot grid across all skills.

Multiple candidates can roll simultaneously. Each gets their character on their phone before the interview begins.

## The Interview

The interview is a real conversation between the existing team and the candidate. It is not a automated quiz — it is a live, facilitated exchange.

**The interviewers** (existing team members or the facilitator acting as hiring manager):
- See the current team's skill coverage and project state on the facilitator screen
- Know which skills and dots are most needed
- Ask questions based on that gap — "do you have experience with API design? infrastructure? security?"

**The candidate**:
- Sees only their own character's dot grid on their phone
- Answers based on what they see — their coloured dots are what they know, their greyed dots are what they don't
- Does not see the team's needs or the facilitator screen

This mirrors a real interview: the team knows what they need, the candidate knows what they have, and the conversation bridges the two. The team cannot see the candidate's full grid — they only learn what they ask about.

## Hiring Decision

After the interview the team decides whether to hire. If multiple candidates are available, they choose between them. The candidate cannot refuse — the decision belongs entirely to the team.

The team may also choose not to hire any candidate — leaving the role open and absorbing the capacity loss.

## After Hiring

Once hired, the new team member's dot grid becomes visible on the facilitator screen. The team may discover the candidate undersold or oversold themselves — just like in real life.
